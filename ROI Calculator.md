Here is a draft of the inputs, the underlying formulas, and how the results could be displayed.

### **Calculator Title:** Calculadora de Crecimiento Bilingüe / Bilingual Growth Calculator

**Introduction:**
"Wondering if a bilingual website is worth it? Answer three simple questions to see the potential impact on your business. Discover how many new customers you could reach in your local area. ¡Sin compromiso!" (No commitment!)

### **Step 1: User Inputs**

We'll ask for three simple pieces of information.

**Input 1: Industry**
*   **Question:** "What is your industry?"
*   **Format:** A dropdown menu.
*   **Options:**
    *   Restaurant / Food Service
    *   Retail / E-commerce
    *   Home Services (Plumbing, Landscaping, etc.)
    *   Professional Services (Law, Accounting, Consulting)
    *   Health & Wellness (Gym, Salon, Spa)
    *   Other

*(**Why we ask this:** Different industries have different levels of online competition and customer search behavior. This allows us to apply a unique "Industry Multiplier" to make the estimate more relevant.)*

**Input 2: Average Monthly Revenue**
*   **Question:** "What is your approximate average monthly revenue?"
*   **Format:** A slider or a field where they can enter a number.
*   **Example:** `$10,000`

*(**Why we ask this:** This is the core metric we'll use to calculate the potential financial uplift. It makes the final result personal and directly tied to their business size.)*

**Input 3: Local Hispanic Population**
*   **Question:** "What is your primary city or service area?"
*   **Format:** A text field where they can type their city (e.g., "Miami, FL").
*   **Behind the Scenes:** This is the clever part. You would use a public data source (like the U.S. Census Bureau API) to automatically fetch the percentage of the Hispanic population for that city. You wouldn't ask the user for the percentage, just their location. This makes it easier for them and the result more credible. If an API is too complex to start, you can begin with a pre-set list of major cities and their percentages.

*(**Why we ask this:** This is the "market opportunity." It directly quantifies the segment of the market they are likely missing by not having a Spanish-language website.)*

---

### **Step 2: The Formulas (The "Magic" Behind the Curtain)**

Here’s how we turn the inputs into a compelling ROI estimate.

**Formula 1: Calculate the Potential New Audience**

`Potential New Audience = (Local Hispanic Population %) x (Industry Multiplier)`

*   **Local Hispanic Population %:** Fetched from the city input. (e.g., Miami is ~70% or 0.70).
*   **Industry Multiplier:** A number between 0 and 1 that we assign based on the industry. This represents how likely a customer in that industry is to search for services online in Spanish.
    *   *Restaurant / Food Service:* **0.8** (High likelihood - people often search for food locally).
    *   *Home Services:* **0.7** (High likelihood).
    *   *Health & Wellness:* **0.6** (Medium-high likelihood).
    *   *Retail / E-commerce:* **0.5** (Medium likelihood).
    *   *Professional Services:* **0.4** (Lower, as referrals can be stronger, but still significant).

**Formula 2: Calculate the Estimated Revenue Increase**

`Estimated Revenue Increase = (Average Monthly Revenue) x (Potential New Audience) x (Conversion Rate)`

*   **Potential New Audience:** The result from Formula 1.
*   **Conversion Rate:** This is a conservative estimate of what percentage of that new audience they might convert into actual revenue. A safe and credible number to start with is **1% to 3% (0.01 to 0.03)**. We can present this as a range.

### **Step 3: Displaying the Results**

Let's walk through an example.

*   **Input:** A **Home Services** business in **Denver, CO** with **$20,000** in monthly revenue.
*   **Behind the Scenes:**
    *   Hispanic population in Denver is ~30% (0.30).
    *   Industry Multiplier for Home Services is 0.7.
    *   Conversion Rate range is 1-3%.

**Calculation:**

1.  `Potential New Audience = 0.30 * 0.7 = 0.21` (or 21%)
2.  `Estimated Revenue Increase (Low End) = $20,000 * 0.21 * 0.01 = $42`
3.  `Estimated Revenue Increase (High End) = $20,000 * 0.21 * 0.03 = $126`

Wait, that number seems too low to be persuasive. Let's adjust. The `Potential New Audience` shouldn't be a direct multiplier on revenue. It should represent the *share of the market* they can now *compete for*. A better approach is to estimate the revenue lift from capturing a small fraction of that new market.

**Let's refine the formula:**

`Estimated Revenue Increase = (Average Monthly Revenue) x (Hispanic Population %) x (Industry Multiplier) x (Capture Rate)`

Let's redefine the multipliers to be more direct.

*   **Hispanic Population %:** 30% (0.30)
*   **Industry Online Search %:** Let's estimate what % of that industry's business comes from online channels. (e.g., Home Services: 50% or 0.5)
*   **Capture Rate:** What % of the *newly available* Spanish-speaking online market could they realistically capture? Let's say **5% to 10%**. This is more intuitive.

**Revised Calculation:**

1.  **Size of Untapped Market (in dollars):** `$20,000 (revenue) * 0.5 (online search %) * 0.30 (Hispanic pop %) = $3,000`
    *(This means there's an estimated $3,000 of their current revenue profile that's tied to a market segment they aren't reaching in their primary language.)* Let's re-think. This is getting complicated.

**Let's Simplify for Maximum Impact.** The logic should be easy to follow.

**Final, Simplified Model:**

1.  **Input 1:** Industry (Dropdown)
2.  **Input 2:** Average number of new customers per month (e.g., 10)
3.  **Input 3:** Average revenue per customer (e.g., $500)

**New Formula:**

`Potential New Spanish-Speaking Customers = (New Customers per Month) x (Hispanic Population %) x (Industry Multiplier)`

`Potential Revenue Increase = (Potential New Customers) x (Average Revenue per Customer)`

**Let's try the Denver example again:**

*   **Inputs:** Home Services, **10** new customers/month, **$2,000** revenue/customer (for a total of $20,000/month). City is Denver.
*   **Data:** Denver Hispanic Pop: 30% (0.30). Industry Multiplier (Home Services): 0.7.

**Calculation:**

1.  `Potential New Customers = 10 * 0.30 * 0.7 = 2.1`
2.  `Potential Revenue Increase = 2.1 * $2,000 = $4,200 per month`

This is a much more powerful and intuitive number! It directly connects to their business growth metrics.

**How to present the final result:**

"Based on your business and your service area (Denver), you're missing out on a market where over 30% of the population is Hispanic.

By launching a bilingual website, you could realistically attract an additional **2 new customers per month**.

For your business, that represents a potential **$4,200 increase in monthly revenue**, or over **$50,000 per year**!

**Ready to capture this market?**"
