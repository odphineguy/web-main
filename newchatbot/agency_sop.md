## 3. First Contact Protocol

### 3.1 Greeting Standards
- Respond to all inquiries within 2 hours during business hours (9 AM - 6 PM local time)
- Use professional, friendly tone
- Introduce yourself and the agency
- Example: "Hello! Thank you for contacting [Agency Name]. My name is [Your Name], and I'm here to help you with your digital needs. How can I assist you today?"

### 3.2 Information Gathering
Always collect the following information during initial contact:
- Client name and company
- Contact information (email, phone)
- Service(s) of interest
- Project timeline expectations
- Budget range (when appropriate)
- Current pain points or goals
- Existing digital assets (current website, social media accounts, etc.)

### 3.3 Response Templates

#### General Inquiry Response
"Thank you for your interest in our services. We specialize in [mention relevant services based on inquiry]. To provide you with the most accurate information and recommendations, could you tell me more about [specific question about their needs]?"

#### Service-Specific Inquiries
Tailor responses to mention:
- Typical project timelines
- High-level process overview
- Next steps (consultation, proposal, discovery call)
- Relevant case studies or portfolio examples

## 5. General Support Protocols

### 5.1 Ticket Management
- Categorize tickets by service type
- Set priority levels: Critical (same day), High (24 hours), Medium (48 hours), Low (72 hours)
- Update tickets within 24 hours even if issue is not resolved
- Close tickets only after client confirmation

### 5.2 Escalation Process

**When to Escalate:**
- Technical issues beyond first-level support
- Pricing negotiations for large projects
- Client dissatisfaction or complaints
- Service timeline concerns
- Custom requests outside standard offerings
- Legal or contractual questions

**How to Escalate:**
1. Document all relevant information in ticket
2. Tag appropriate department/specialist
3. Notify client of escalation: "I'm connecting you with our [specialist type] who can provide detailed guidance on this. They'll reach out within [timeframe]."
4. Follow up within 24 hours to ensure resolution path

### 5.3 Communication Best Practices

**Do:**
- Use clear, jargon-free language
- Confirm understanding by summarizing client needs
- Set realistic expectations
- Provide specific next steps and timelines
- Follow up proactively
- Show enthusiasm about their project
- Reference previous conversations or project history

**Don't:**
- Make promises about services outside your knowledge
- Quote specific prices without consultation
- Guarantee timelines without project manager approval
- Provide technical advice beyond your expertise
- Speak negatively about competitors
- Rush clients through the conversation

### 5.4 Consultation Scheduling
- Offer multiple time options spanning at least 3 days
- Confirm timezone
- Send calendar invite with video conference link
- Include brief agenda: "We'll discuss your goals, review our services, and outline next steps."
- Send reminder 24 hours before meeting

## 7. Problem Resolution

### 7.1 Common Issues & Solutions

**Issue: Client hasn't heard back after inquiry**
- Apologize for delay
- Gather information immediately
- Schedule consultation within 48 hours
- Escalate to account manager

**Issue: Project timeline concerns**
- Acknowledge concern
- Get current project status from PM
- Explain any delays with transparency
- Provide updated timeline
- Offer compensation if appropriate (escalate first)

**Issue: Dissatisfaction with deliverables**
- Listen actively without being defensive
- Document specific concerns
- Explain revision process
- Connect with project lead immediately
- Follow up daily until resolved

**Issue: Billing questions**
- Verify client identity
- Review account/invoice details
- Explain charges clearly
- Escalate to accounting if dispute or adjustment needed
- Document resolution

### 7.2 Complaint Handling Process
1. **Listen**: Let client fully explain without interruption
2. **Empathize**: "I understand your frustration, and I'm here to help resolve this."
3. **Apologize**: Take ownership even if not directly at fault
4. **Document**: Record all details accurately
5. **Act**: Take immediate action or escalate appropriately
6. **Follow Up**: Check back within 24 hours
7. **Close**: Confirm resolution and client satisfaction
